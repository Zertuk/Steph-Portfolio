About Me

I am a senior at NDSU, pursuing a Bachelor in Fine Arts degree in Theatre Performance. My focus is on straight theatre, with an emphasis on classical literature. In the summer of 2011, I traveled abroad in London with the American Institute for Foreign Study. There, I studied Shakespeare at the Globe Theatre. More recently, I was employed by the Brownville Village Theatre for its 2012 and 2013 seasons. I performed in three of the five shows each year, makeup and hair designed all ten shows, and was the script supervisor for the 2013 show You're a Good Man, Charlie Brown. I also makeup and hair designed all five shows that season.

In Mach of 2013, I worked on my first personal project in fulfillment of my senior capstone at NDSU. For the capstone, I performed an abridged version of Medea. I compressed the script, found a director and lighting designer, and assembled a cast. I also put together the necessary arrangements to publicly perform, designed and constructed the set, and starred as the title character. It was hard work, but it was extremely rewarding. Through collaboration with the others involved, we were able to put together something to be proud of.

I've been involved in many shows at NDSU. My acting credits include (but are not limited to) the title role in Miss Julie, Aunt Eller in Oklahoma!, and an ogre/chorus member in Wokashi (a collection of Kyogen comedies directed by Tokuro Miyake, from the Izumi School in Japan). I also designed the makeup and hair for NDSU's Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street as well as The Wild Party and A Servant of Two Masters. I have experience as an assistant stage manager as well, when I held that position in Newfangled Theatre's production of Treefall. For three consecutive years, I worked in the costume shop at the university.

Medea Monologue

My 4th year at North Dakota State University, I worked on a Capstone Project to showcase what I had learned throughout the years. For this project, I compressed Euripides' Medea, found a director and lighting designer, and assembled a cast. I also put together the necessary arrangements to publicly perform, and starred as the title character. In this scene, Medea battles with the choice she has made to kill her children, as part of an overall master plan to get revenge on Jason.
